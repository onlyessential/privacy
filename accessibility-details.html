Prominent Disclosure

AppLocker uses Android’s Accessibility Service only to detect when a protected app is opened and to display a lock screen for your security.

We do not read, collect, store, or share your on-screen content or personal data. No advertising or tracking uses this permission.

Purpose & Scope

  • Detects foreground app transitions (e.g., when you open an app you’ve protected).
  • Shows a secure lock screen overlay when a protected app is detected.
  • Used strictly for app-lock functionality; no other automation or assistance features.

Data Handling

  • Processes minimal event data such as the package name of the foreground app.
  • Event data is handled locally on your device; it is not transmitted to our servers.
  • No screenshots, key presses, text, or content from your screen are captured or stored.

Consent & Control

  • Enabling the service is optional and requires your explicit consent in-app (checkbox + “I Agree”).
  • Navigating away (back/home/tap outside) is treated as no consent.
  • You can disable the permission anytime in system settings.

How to Enable / Disable

  1. Open Settings → Accessibility.
  2. Find AppLocker under downloaded apps.
  3. Toggle the service On to enable or Off to disable.

Exact path may vary by device/OS version.

Why We Need Accessibility (Instead of Other APIs)

Android restricts background detection of which app is currently in the foreground. Accessibility events allow AppLocker to reliably know when a protected app becomes active so we can present the lock screen at the right moment.

Your Choices

  • Use AppLocker without the Accessibility permission (core app works, app-locking features may be limited).
  • Enable the permission for full app-locking protection.
  • Revoke the permission anytime in system settings.
